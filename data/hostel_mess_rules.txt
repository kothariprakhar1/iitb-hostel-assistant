IIT Bombay Hostel Mess Rules (Source: Gymkhana Hostel 10, IIT Bombay)

1. Mess timings
The mess timings will be very strictly followed, and no request will be entertained outside of the mess timings.

2. Mess menu items
Only items mentioned in the menu will be served, and no demand can be made for any item which is not on the menu that day. The same applies to Jain and the boiled counter.

3. Non-messing and rebate
Any student can choose not to register for mess facility permanently for a semester; in such a case, Rs. 1300 per month will be deducted as compensation. The student may continue eating by paying for an individual meal on a normal per-day cost applicable to other students. The remaining amount from their mess bills would be refunded at the end of the semester. Non-messing is not applicable during the vacation period; it is only an option during the semester.

The total number of days allowed for rebate is 15 per semester. Students should apply for a rebate at least 2 full days in advance and for a minimum of 3 days. For academic leave, 2 copies of the duly signed department leave form should be attached with the rebate form 2 days in advance of travel.

4. Mess utensils
Taking mess utensils outside the mess area is strictly not allowed. Violating the rules leads to fines: Rs. 500 per utensil for the first complaint, Rs. 1000 per utensil for the second complaint, and revocation of mess facilities for more than 2 violations.

Students are not allowed to share plates in the mess for any reason.

5. Guests
Guest entry registers or a cash coupon system should be used when bringing friends or relatives to eat in the mess.

6. Medical leave
In case of medical leave, students can have food delivered to their room. Medical proof of illness and the number of days required must be submitted to the hall manager. Mess staff will bring food to the room and collect plates afterward.

7. Behavior
Misbehavior with mess workers or council members is not tolerated. Complaints regarding mess issues should be directed to the mess council rather than arguing with staff. Serious misbehavior complaints are forwarded to the disciplinary committee.
