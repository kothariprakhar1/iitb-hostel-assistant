OFFICE OF THE DEAN OF STUDENT AFFAIRS (DoSA)

The Office of the Dean of Student Affairs (DoSA) is responsible for the welfare of students, encompassing administration of hostels and students' non-academic activities including sports, cultural, and technical activities. These tasks are carried out primarily by three bodies:

1. Hostel Coordinating Unit (HCU)
2. Student Gymkhana
3. Student Wellness Centre (SWC)

Hostel Coordinating Unit (HCU): Operates under the guidance of the Associate Dean of Student Affairs, coordinating with all hostel wardens, hostel managers, and associated staff. Wardens of the various hostels are faculty members of IIT Bombay, while hostel managers are staff members of the institute. Day-to-day activities of the hostels are looked after by the hostel managers and an elected hostel council of students, led by the General Secretary of the respective hostel.

Students' Gymkhana: Has a group of qualified Sports Officers and other instructors to run activities, coordinated by three faculty members who officiate as Chairperson (Sports), Chairperson (Cultural), and Chairperson (Technical). An apex group of elected General Secretaries (covering sports, cultural, technical, academic, and hostel affairs) plays a key role in coordinating activities and representing students.

Student Wellness Centre (SWC): Acts as a key support system for students' mental well-being. Headed by a Chairperson who is a faculty member, with day-to-day operations looked after by the in-charge SWC.

For First Year Students / Freshers: DoSA maintains a dedicated "First Year Students" resource section and a Parents Handbook, intended to help freshers and their families navigate hostel life and campus systems when they first arrive.

Support systems freshers can access, coordinated via DoSA:
- Gender Cell
- Hospital
- Language Resource (LRWC)
- PwD Cell (Persons with Disabilities)
- SC/ST Cell
- Student Wellness Centre (SWC)
