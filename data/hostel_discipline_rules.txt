IIT Bombay General Hostel Discipline and Conduct Rules (Source: Gymkhana Hostel 10)

1. Behavior
Residents shall not participate in any anti-national, anti-social, or other undesirable activities. All residents shall be courteous and fair with everyone, inside and outside the hostel. Misbehavior toward the PHO (Presiding Hostel Officer), mess staff, office staff, security, or council members shall not be tolerated.

Ragging is a criminal offence. Students should not screen pirated or unauthorized movies. All students must carry their institute identity cards and produce them on demand. Furniture and fitments should not be moved from their designated place. Walls, doors, shelves, and windows should not be defaced from inside or outside the hostel.

Any misbehavior complaints will be forwarded to the disciplinary committee. Fines and/or disciplinary punishments awarded under the WDC (Warden's Disciplinary Committee) and DAC (Disciplinary Action Committee) must be adhered to within the given time frame.

2. Smoking and drinking
Possession, smoking, and consumption of alcoholic drinks and/or narcotic drugs inside IIT Bombay is strictly prohibited.

3. Pets
Petting or feeding dogs/cats inside the hostel premises, or assisting their entrance into the hostel, is prohibited and subject to fines.

4. Hostel accommodation overview
IIT Bombay has 17 hostels for student accommodation. Hostels 1 to 9, 12, 13, 14, and 15 (A and B wing) are for boys, while Hostels 10, 10A, 11, and 15 (C wing) are for girls. Every hostel has washing and ironing facilities, a computer room, a TV, sports grounds, and indoor games such as table tennis, carrom, and chess. Access to these facilities is generally granted against a student's I-Card. Each room has internet connectivity, and several hostels have shops providing daily necessities, stationery, xerox, and printing facilities.

5. Administration
The Dean of Student Affairs (DoSA) office is responsible for student welfare, including hostel administration, and non-academic activities such as sports, cultural, and technical events. These responsibilities are carried out primarily through the Hostel Coordinating Unit (HCU), the Student Gymkhana, and the Student Wellness Centre (SWC).
